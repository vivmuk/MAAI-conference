Good Prompt (CRAFT)

Context: Follow-up email to an oncologist interested in real-world evidence. Role: Act as an MSL communication coach. Action: Generate 5 subject line options focused on what this HCP cares about most. Format: Bullet dots with 5 options under 8 words. Tone: short, specific, open-worthy.

Bad Prompt (vague)

"Give me email subjects."

Missing: context, role, action specifics, format, tone.

Good Prompt (CRAFT)

Context: KOL meeting about Phase 3 cardiovascular outcomes data. Role: Act as a Medical Affairs meeting strategist. Action: Generate 4 discussion angles for this meeting. Format: Bullet dots with clear, actionable angles. Tone: strategic, clinically relevant. Focus on what a cardiologist would find most valuable.

Bad Prompt (vague)

"What should I talk about?"

Good Prompt (CRAFT)

Context: Addressing HCP objection about trial population not matching their patient mix. Role: Act as an MSL objection coach. Action: Generate 3 ways to address this objection. Format: Bullet dots with data-driven responses. Tone: confident, empathetic, evidence-based.

Bad Prompt (vague)

"How do I respond to objections?"

Missing: context, role, action specifics, format, tone.

Pro tips

Constrain the output.

Ask for a specific count and length so ideas are usable quickly.

Anchor the audience.

State the exact HCP type and what they care about most.

Ask for variety.

Request distinct angles to avoid near-duplicate options.
