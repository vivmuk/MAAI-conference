Superpower 4

Provide Feedback

Get coaching to improve tone, clarity, and reply likelihood before you send.

Practice Scenarios

Select a scenario below to practice. Click "Use This Prompt" to load it into the chat.

Good Prompt (CRAFT)

Context: Follow-up email to an oncologist after a congress meeting. Role: Act as my MSL email communication coach. Action: Give me 3 specific improvements that would increase reply likelihood - tone, clarity, or relevance - and explain why. Format: Bullet dots with improvement and short rationale. Tone: constructive, specific.

Bad Prompt (vague)

"Give me feedback on this."

Missing: context, role, action specifics, format, tone.

Good Prompt (CRAFT)

Context: Preparing talking points for a meeting with an academic KOL in cardiology. Role: Act as an executive presentation coach. Action: Review these talking points for a KOL meeting. Rate clarity (1-5) and suggest how to sound more confident and data-driven. Format: Clarity rating, then bullet dots with specific improvements. Tone: direct, coaching.

Bad Prompt (vague)

"How can I improve this?"

Missing: context, role, action specifics, format, tone.

Good Prompt (CRAFT)

Context: Territory update message to Medical Affairs leadership. Role: Coach me on executive presence. Action: Review this message and tell me where I sound uncertain, too wordy, or miss opportunities to demonstrate expertise. Format: Bullet dots with specific issues and how to fix them. Tone: direct, constructive.

Bad Prompt (vague)

"Does this sound professional?"

Missing: context, role, action specifics, format, tone.

Pro tips

Ask for reasons.

Explanations teach the team how to improve future drafts.

Limit the number of changes.

Three improvements keeps feedback actionable.

Prioritize the goal.

Make the ask and relevance clear to increase reply rates.
