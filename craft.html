CRAFT Framework

CRAFT gives AI the context and constraints it needs. Use the full structure to get consistent, high-quality outputs.

CRAFT in one glance

  1. Context: Who, why, and what matters.
  2. Role: The expert voice to adopt.
  3. Action: The exact task.
  4. Format: The output structure.
  5. Tone: The style and formality.

Detailed explanation

Use each element to remove ambiguity and avoid rework.

C - Context

Describe the situation, stakeholder, and goal. Mention why it matters and any constraints like timing or compliance.

Example: "Cardiology KOL follow-up after ACC; short email to request a 15-minute call."

R - Role

Assign a role so the output matches your standards. This keeps tone consistent and aligned to Medical Affairs expectations.

Example: "Act as an MSL communication coach."

A - Action

State the task in clear action verbs: analyze, rewrite, generate, summarize, or critique.

Example: "Rewrite the email into three bullets and a one-line ask."

F - Format

Lock in the output structure so it is immediately usable and easy to scan.

Example: "Return three bullets plus a one-line ask."

T - Tone

Set the style so the AI matches your voice, audience, and level of formality.

Example: "Warm, concise, and professional."

CRAFT template

Context: [Describe the situation, audience, and goal]

Role: [Who should the AI act as?]

Action: [What should it do?]

Format: [What format should it return?]

Tone: [What style should it use?]
