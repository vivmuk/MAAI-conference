Good Prompt (CRAFT)

Context: Follow-up email to a busy endocrinologist about Phase 3 diabetes data. Role: Act as an MSL communication reviewer. Action: Review this email and flag where it could be misunderstood, unclear, too long, or low-value for this HCP type. Suggest concise fixes. Format: Bullet dots with issue and fix. Tone: direct, helpful.

Bad Prompt (vague)

"Is this email good?"

Missing: context, role, action specifics, format, tone.

Good Prompt (CRAFT)

Context: Preparing to discuss Phase 3 NSCLC trial data with a community oncologist. Role: Act as a critical HCP reviewing this data. Action: Identify 3 potential gaps or questions an HCP might raise about this trial summary. Consider patient population, endpoints, and follow-up period. Format: Bullet dots with specific concerns. Tone: skeptical but fair.

Bad Prompt (vague)

"Analyze this trial data."

Good Prompt (CRAFT)

Context: Preparing for a KOL meeting where competitive questions may arise. Role: Play the role of a competitor MSL. Action: What weaknesses would you highlight in this data presentation? What questions would challenge our positioning? Format: Bullet dots with specific competitive challenges. Tone: direct, challenging.

Pro tips

Name the failure modes.

Ask for ambiguity, compliance risk, missing context, and low-value lines.

Request fixes, not just critique.

Always ask for concise revisions so output is ready to use.

Keep the audience explicit.

HCP, internal, or leadership changes what is considered clear and valuable.
